Recent advances in image classification highlight the limitations of traditional convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in capturing complex spatial relationships, as well as the limitations of Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) models that rely on simple routing mechanisms. At the same time, quantum computing introduces a new paradigm with high expressive potential for decision-making.
Motivated by these gaps, this work explores whether combining graph-based representations, adaptive expert models, and quantum routing can improve performance. Specifically, we investigate a unified framework that integrates graph neural networks (GNNs), MoE, and a quantum router to enable better representation and more effective expert selection.
This research aims to evaluate whether quantum-enhanced routing can provide measurable benefits in classification tasks, particularly for medical images.
